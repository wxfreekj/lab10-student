üìö Introduction

Severe weather poses significant threats to lives and property each year. Among these threats, tornadoes are particularly dangerous due to their sudden development, powerful winds, and potential for widespread destruction. Understanding how and why tornadoes form can help us prepare and respond more effectively.

This lab will explore the meteorological ingredients required for tornado formation through interpretation of various weather maps and show you how meteorologists utilize Doppler radar to detect and track tornadoes.

For this lab, you will analyze the Hallam tornado event, one of the widest tornadoes ever recorded at 2.5 miles wide. By examining weather conditions before and during the storm, you'll learn how meteorologists predict and monitor severe weather events.

üîë Key Ingredients for Tornado Formation

  • Strong Winds at 500 hPa: Winds greater than 40 knots provide the necessary upper-level support for severe thunderstorms
  • Wind Shear: Significant changes in wind speed and direction with altitude can promote rotation in thunderstorms
  • Moisture: Surface dewpoints greater than 65¬∞F supply the moisture needed for thunderstorms to develop and intensify
  • Trigger Mechanisms: Features such as fronts or areas where different air masses converge (the "triple point") initiate storm formation
